Density Estimation on Compact Metric Spaces using Sieve Priors

This is based on Section 3 of our paper Bayesian Nonparametrics and Sieve Priors for Directional Statistics, which can be accessed on arXiv. Please refer to that document for complete explanations and references.

Consider the problem of estimating an unknown density \(f_0\) defined on a (finitely measured) compact metric space \((\mathbb{M}, d)\) using an i.i.d. sample \(X_i \sim f_0\), \(i \in \{1,2,\dots, k\}\). This is of particular interest in the context of circular statistics and statistics on manifolds, where \(\mathbb{M}\) could be the circle, the sphere or some other compact Riemannian surface. Applications range from the analysis of seasonal and angular measurements to the statistics of shapes and configurations.

The goal here is to provide a general framework and theory for a Bayesian nonparametric approach to this problem. Here we focus on sieve priors, some instances of which are also Dirichlet Process Mixtures and Random Series with a random number of components.

Sieve priors

Assume that \(f_0 \in \mathbb{F}\), where \(\mathbb{F}\) is some arbitrary set of densities on \(\mathbb{M}\) (with respect to the aforementioned finite measure). We break up \(\mathbb{F}\) into sets \(\mathcal{S}_n\), \(n\in \mathbb{N}\), called sieves and such that

\[ \mathbb{F} = \overline{\bigcup_{n} \mathcal{S}_n}, \]

where the overline represents \(L^1\) closure in \(\mathbb{F}\). Given priors \(\Pi_n\) on \(\mathcal{S}_n\) and a distribution \(\rho\) on \(\mathbb{N}\), we then obtain a sieve prior \(\Pi\) as

\[ \Pi = \sum_{n \in \mathbb{N}} \rho(n) \Pi_n. \]

For many useful statistical models, the sieves \(\mathcal{S}_n\) appear as the images \(\mathcal{S}_n = T_n(\mathbb{F})\) of positive linear operators \(T_n: L^1(\mathbb{M}) \rightarrow L^1(\mathbb{M})\). This is the case, for instance, with the Bernstein-Dirichlet prior of Petrone and Wasserman (2002) and its multivariate variants.

Here we state posterior consistency and convergence rates results in terms of properties of these operators and simple conditions on \(\rho\) and \(\Pi_n\).

Posterior consistency

Let \(\mathbb{F}\) be the set of all bounded densities on the finitely measured compact metric space \((\mathbb{M}, d)\). We assume that \(T_n:L^1(\mathbb{M}) \rightarrow L^1(\mathbb{M})\) is a sequence of linear operators mapping densities to densities and that \(\Pi_n\) are priors on \(T_n(\mathbb{F})\).

Theorem 3.3 Let \(\mathbb{F}\), \(\Pi_n\), \(\Pi\) and \(T_n\) be as above. Suppose that \(T_n(\mathbb{F})\subset \mathbb{F}\) are of finite dimensions bounded by an increasing sequence \(d_n\in \mathbb{N}\), and also that \(\|T_nf-f\|_{\infty}\to 0\), \(n\to \infty\), for every continuous function \(f\) on \(\mathbb{M}\). If \(0< \rho(n)< c e^{-Cd_n}\), for some \(c >0\), \(C > 0\) and if \(\Pi_n\) has support \(T_n(\mathbb{F})\), then the posterior distribution of \(\Pi\) is strongly consistent on \(\mathbb{F}\).

Note that no continuity or positivity assumption is made here on the density \(f_0\).

Adaptative posterior contraction rates

In order to obtain contraction rates, the following assumptions are made on the sequence of operators \(T_n\) and on the distribution \(\rho\) which induces the prior \(\Pi = \sum_n \rho(n) \Pi_n\) defined as before, with \(\Pi_n\) priors on \(T_n(\mathbb{F})\).

  1. The sequence of linear operators \(T_n : L^1(\mathbb{M}) \rightarrow L^1(\mathbb{M})\) with \(T_n(\mathbb{F}) \subset \mathbb{F}\) maps densities to densities and is such that \(\|T_n 1 - 1\|_\infty \rightarrow 0\) for the constant function \(1\).

  2. There exists \(d_n \in \mathbb{N}\) an increasing integer sequence with \(d_n \geq \dim(T_n(\mathbb{F}))\) and satisfying \(d_n \asymp n^d\) for some \(d \geq 1\).

  3. The distribution \(\rho\) on \(\mathbb{N}\) satisfies \(\log(\rho(n)) \asymp -d_n \log(d_n)\).

Theorem 3.5 Suppose that 1.-3. are satisfied. Let \(f_0\in \mathbb{F}\) be such that \(\|\log f_0\|_\infty < \infty\), \(\|T_n f_0 - f_0\|_\infty = \mathcal{O}(n^{-\beta})\) for some \(\beta > 0\) and suppose there exists \(\kappa > 0\), \(\varepsilon_0 > 0\) such that for every \(n \in \mathbb{N}\) and every \(0 < \varepsilon < \varepsilon_0 / d_n\),

\[ \Pi_n\left(\left\{ f \in T_n(\mathbb{F}) : \| f - T_n f_0 \|_\infty \leq \varepsilon \right\}\right) \geq \left(\varepsilon/d_n\right)^{\kappa d_n}. \]

Then the posterior distribution of \(\Pi\) contracts around \(f_0\) at the rate \((n/\log(n))^{-\beta/(2\beta + d)}\).

Remark

When \(\mathbb{M}\) has a well-defined Minkowski dimension \(m \geq 1\), we can easily define operators \(T_n\) satisfying assumption 1 and such that both \(\|T_n f - f\|_\infty = \mathcal{O}(\omega_f(1/n))\) and \(\dim T_n(\mathbb{F}) \asymp n^m\), where \(\omega_f\) is the modulus of continuity of \(f \in \mathcal{C}(\mathbb{M})\). In this case, we can let \(d = m\) in assumption 2. If \(f\) satisfies the Holder smoothness condition \(\omega_f(\delta) \leq C \delta^\beta\) for \(\beta \in (0,1]\) and some \(C > 0\), then Theorem 3.5 yields the rate \((n/\log(n))^{-\beta/(2\beta + m)}\).
